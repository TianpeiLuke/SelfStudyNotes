The following summarize the ultimate mission for Context-Enhanced Buyer Abuse Prevention (COAP) in long term. 

1. **Voice of Customer (VOC):**  COAP can listen to and detect buyer abuse event based on feedbacks from both buyer and seller customer. Listening to voice of customers allows us to get direct access to the first hand evidence that support our decision on buyer's refund/return requests.  

2. **Context Enhancement**: COAP augments customer transaction history with additional contextual information from customer contacts to gain comprehensive picture behinds concessions. It also helps to correct noisy annotations and inconsistent attributes from transaction table. As a result, it will lead to an improvement in decision accuracy in risk evaluation and an improved buyer and seller experience on Amazon.  

3. **Customer Experience Optimization:** COAP directly optimize the customer experience based on voice of customer and enhanced context without relying on seller or Customer Service agents.
    1. **Reactionary Enforcement:**  Establish a reactionary enforcement mechanism that allows the system and human investigator to react to the customer feedback and take enforcement effectively. **Decision automation** is preferred to scale up the capacity.  
     
    2. **Seller Perception Index (SPI) Improvement:** Improve seller perception towards Amazon by addressing the most concerned issue in seller feedback and referral channel such as harassment, threatening, review bombing, hate speech/offensive languages;  
       

Ideally, we would like COAP to be a system that **translates** buyer's request or seller's complaints into **actionable tasks** with **enhanced evidence available from both buyer and seller side in order to support or against it.**
