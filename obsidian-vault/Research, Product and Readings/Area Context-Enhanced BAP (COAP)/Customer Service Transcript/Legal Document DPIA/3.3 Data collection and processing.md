At gather phase, we collect response (SNS messages) from [Junction service](https://w.amazon.com/bin/view/CS_Neuron_and_Wit/Neuron/Junction/) owned by CS Neuron Team. The output fields are listed in section 3.2.

At processing phase, we adopt the standard natural language processing methods to parse text, filter irrelevant sentence and remove sensitive information.

1. Contact Group Identification and Filtering: In this step, we separate the human-human interations from human-bot interations as we only focus on human-human interations.

2.  Sentence Filtering: we further remove sentences in raw transcript that are not related to refund and return events.

3. Sentence Grouping: we then group sentence** by different parties, and drop sentences from Amazon agent.

4. Customer-Identifying Data Removal: we detect customer name, address, locations, organization as well as other customer information in the transcript and we remove them or mask them with common tokens (e.g. using ‘Name’ in place of real customer name).

5. Text Normalization: using other ad-hoc rules to normalize the text format of dialogue.

Finally, after text processing, we are feeding transformed text into natural language understanding models. Additional steps in model inference are

1. Tokenization
2. Text Embedding
3. Abuse Risk Level Categorization / Abuse Event Summarization.  

The model inference results would be used for human investigation.
