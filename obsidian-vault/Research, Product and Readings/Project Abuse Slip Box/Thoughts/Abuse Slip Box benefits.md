The benefits of using the Slip Box can also become the benefits of using the Abuse Slip Box. After all, evaluation and investigation are hardly linear processes. Evidences for abuse classification would arrive at different time. Every time when a new evidence arrives, a re-evaluation process would be triggered. Such re-evaluation could also be triggered by escalation and referrals from external customers such as buyer and 3rd party seller, or from internal teams. Handling the _non-linear re-evaluation process_ is a built-in feature for Abuse Slip Box due to its network-based organization and atomic note structure.   
  
Another benefits of using Abuse Slip Box is to **improve label quality** before the training phase starts. Incorrect labels in training set would cause increased false positives and false negatives in model decision at inference time. An example of such label cleaning process is the abuse reversal and reclassification, where human or machine would utilize additional contexts from customer contacts to correct abuse reason codes. Abuse Slip Box can be used to match cases of similar context with their annotations. It also associates decisions with SOPs. Through graph connections and context summarizations, we would see the inconsistency in system/human decision making, spotting the missing piece during the evaluation and to correct them via label propagation.   
  
The most important benefit of using Abuse Slip Box is its emphasis on **context summarization and comparison**. Similar as the impact of original Slip Box to human readers, using Abuse Slip Box would allow us to keep only the essence of context and to remove irrelevant or redundant information. It is expected that through summarization, both ML model and human investigator would improve their decision accuracy. We propose to use LLM to achieve this context summarization and comparison. It is known that due to emergent ability of LLM, the performance of text and dialogue summarization has increased drastically to human level. LLM also has deomstrated its capability to list the similarities of two text paragraphs.
