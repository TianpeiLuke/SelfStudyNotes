## What is BSM ?

**Buyer-Seller Messaging (BSM)** is an *email and chat service* that enables and maintains contacts between Amazon buyer and Amazon seller regarding the product information, delivery notification, refund/return requests and service maintenance, etc. 

According to Amazon policy, to obtain concessions in MFN from 3rd party seller, buyers are expected to contact seller first and start negotiation before Amazon get involved. Usually, buyer will receive refunds or be allowed to return after contacting with seller directly. If the seller disapproves the concession or not replied for 48 hours, buyer is allowed to file the A-Z claim to Amazon to determine if the buyer is eligible for refunds.

## What to do with BSM?

With the Buyer-Seller Messaging Service, you can do the following:

- View past communication with sellers on Amazon.com.
- Reply to seller messages.
- Send attachments to sellers. File types supported include plain text (.txt), PDF, images (.jpg, .gif, and .png), and Word documents in .doc and .docx format. The message can't exceed 10 MB (including attachments).


>[!quote]
>**Note:**
> - We reserve the right to delete or remove any attachment containing inappropriate content. We reserve the right to decide what is inappropriate content.
> - We reserve the right to delete or remove any email addresses or links to untrusted websites from the message. We reserve the right to determine which websites aren't trusted.
> - You can use any information the seller provides to contact the seller. However, we recommend that you use the [Message Center](https://www.amazon.com/gp/message). This service ensures that emails are filtered for spam and phishing attempts. **We'll also keep a copy of all correspondence, which can be used to support any [A-to-z Guarantee](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=GQ37ZCNECJKTFYQV) claims you submit.** [^1] If an email you send to the seller bounces, the seller can still view it in the Seller Communication Center. Sellers usually respond to your emails within two days.
> - You can opt out of unsolicited messages from third-party sellers. To do this, update your promotional email settings under [Email Preferences](https://www.amazon.com/gp/cpc/homepage?ref=gss_to_cpc).

## When BSM is eligible?

Third-party sellers can be contacted in two ways: *Pre-order* via the **product detail page** and *post-order* via [Your Orders](https://www.amazon.com/gp/css/order-history). Before contacting a third-party seller, check if the listing is eligible for Amazon Prime or if the seller participates in *Customer Service* by Amazon. If so, Amazon [Customer Service](https://www.amazon.com/hz/contact-us/foresight/hubgateway) is happy to help answer your questions.

**Contacting a seller post-order:**

1. Go to [Your Orders](https://www.amazon.com/gp/css/order-history).
2. Find your order in the list.
3. Select *Problem with order*.
4. Choose your topic from list displayed.
5. Select *Contact seller.*

Allow two days for the seller to respond. In most cases, you'll receive a reply sooner.

**Contacting a seller pre-order:**

1. On the product detail page, select the name of the seller.
2. On the next page, select *Ask a Question*.
3. Select the appropriate options in the *Seller Messaging Assistant chat* or fill out the contact form.
4. Your message will be sent to the seller via the **Buyer-Seller Messaging Service**. They'll respond to you by email within two business days.

**If the seller does not respond**

Most orders placed with sellers are covered by our A-to-z Guarantee. If you don't hear from the seller within two days, go to [A-to-z Guarantee](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=GQ37ZCNECJKTFYQV) to see your options.
