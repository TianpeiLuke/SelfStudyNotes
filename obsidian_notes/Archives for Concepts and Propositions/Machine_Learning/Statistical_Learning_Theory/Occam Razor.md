## Concept Definition

>[!important]
>**Name**: Occam's Razor

>[!important] Occam's Razor
>
>A **short explanation** (that is, a hypothesis that has a short length) tends to be *more valid* than a long explanation.

>[!important] Definition
>This is a well known principle, called **Occamâ€™s razor**, after William of Ockham, a 14th-century English logician, who is believed to have been the first to phrase it explicitly.
